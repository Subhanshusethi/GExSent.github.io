A NOVEL FRAMEWORK FOR MULTIMODAL SENTIMENT ANALYSIS

GExSent: Gated Experts for Robust Sentiment Analysis

Published at the International Joint Conference on Neural Networks (IJCNN)

Read The Paper View Code

Abstract

The field of multimodal sentiment analysis has gained huge attention through its broad applications in different forms of communication. In this study, a novel architecture, GExSent, is proposed that combines the powerful encoding capabilities of CLIP and Modern BERT. Furthermore, it introduces two feature enhancement modules—Hierarchical Gated Mixture of Experts (H-GMoE) and a Gated Attention Mechanism (GAM)—to support task-specific learning and enhance the relevance of multimodal features. Experimental results on benchmark datasets, including MOSI, MOSEI, and Memotion, demonstrate superior performance when compared to current state-of-the-art methods.

The GExSent Architecture

Our model intelligently fuses visual and textual data through a three-stage framework, ensuring that the most relevant information is prioritized for accurate sentiment prediction.

Fig 1: GExSent Full Architecture

This diagram shows the end-to-end flow from unimodal inputs to the final classification head.

1. Unimodal Representation

We leverage powerful pre-trained models: CLIP extracts robust visual features, while Modern BERT captures deep contextual understanding from text.

2. Hierarchical Gated MoE

This module acts as an intelligent filter, selecting the most informative "expert" tokens from each modality and reducing noise for cleaner feature representation.

3. Gated Attention (GAM)

GAM dynamically modulates the interaction between modalities, applying adaptive gates to ensure the final representation is context-aware and robust.

Conclusion & Future Work

Our work introduces a significant step toward more adaptive and interpretable multimodal sentiment analysis. By using H-GMOE and GAM, GExSent dynamically adapts to the significance of each modality.

Future work will explore extending these capabilities to finer-grained emotion classification and incorporating additional modalities such as audio.
